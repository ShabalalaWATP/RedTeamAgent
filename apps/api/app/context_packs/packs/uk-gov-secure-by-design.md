# UK Government Secure by Design

Use this pack when a review asks for secure-by-design assurance, UK government alignment, critical service readiness or accountable risk ownership.

## Review Rubric

- Confirm that security is treated as an early design constraint, not a late test activity.
- Identify the named service owner, risk owner and technical owner for each material risk.
- Check that risk ownership, risk acceptance and residual risk are explicit and auditable.
- Check that security controls are proportionate to service criticality, data sensitivity and user harm.
- Look for defence in depth: identity, access control, logging, monitoring, backup, recovery and incident response.
- Prefer simple secure defaults over optional controls that depend on perfect user behaviour.
- Confirm that delivery teams can evidence threat modelling, secure development practice, testing and operational monitoring.
- Require rollback, recovery and vulnerability-response paths before irreversible launch.

## Output Requirements

- Separate design gaps from implementation bugs.
- Label any claim that depends on policy interpretation as a governance judgement.
- Do not claim government compliance unless the evidence proves the relevant controls.
